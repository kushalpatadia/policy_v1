Contractor Buy to Let Mortgages

As a contractor, you are looking to invest that money into a BTL property.

Obtaining a Buy To Let Mortgage is no easy feat due to the various calculation rates with all the lenders, the rental stress, portfolio lending and various other points, please do not make the mistake that many others make and get the wrong adviser, who does not really get the lenders who will lend to Contractor’s on a day rate.

You will also need a mortgage broker who understands and specialises in the lender policies and all the different stress calculations in order to place you with the right lender at the best interest rate possible. Buy to let mortgages are assessed utilising the rental income of the subject property and this needs to be demonstrated to the lender that even in extreme condition sthe rent will finance the buy to let mortgage.

One thing that is very popular with high earners is Limited Company BTL due to the tax changes, our brokers understand how these work and which lenders are best to place these with.

You should get specialist advice from your accountant as to which vehicle is best, the sole trader or limited company approach when deciding how to progress with your investment

Our specialist contractor mortgage brokers can help you get the mortgage that you need.

1. Speak to a specialist contractor adviser

Who will secure you an agreement in principle with a lender, this will help you negotiate and make an offer on a property.

2. Contact your dedicated adviser

Once you have agreed a purchase price for the property, you should speak to your adviser and your application will formally begin.

3. During your application

Your dedicated adviser will request documents from you in order to ensure that your application can be presented to the lender in a manner that will be acceptable to that particular lenders underwriter, each lender has their own process and criteria

4. Valuation on the subject property by the lender is carried out

The lender will want to make sure that the property that it will be lending on will be sufficient security, so it will want its own surveyor to go out and view the property.

5. Your mortgage offer will be produced

Once all the lenders searches and surveys have been completed, the lender will issue you with a formal mortgage offer.

6. Your solicitor will request your deposit money from you and exchange contracts.

Your lender will only release funds to a solicitor that is also on the panel of that particular lender, please check with your adviser at Contractor Mortgages Direct who will be able to recommend a solicitor for you. It is at this point of exchange that you should ensure you have adequate buildings insurance in place.

7. You will agree a completion date.

Completion Date can be agreed generally after the exchange of contracts has taken place, this is where the solicitors take instruction for you and the seller regarding a suitable date for completion and terms are agreed for for exchange of contracts, this is post all necesary checks have taken place on title of property and enquries raised have come back satisfactory A date for completion will be set and then when the day finally arrives.

8. Congratulations!
